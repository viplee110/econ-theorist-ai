## Short Readable Draft

Public recognition can make team members work harder while also changing which
tasks they choose. The toy mechanism is that recognition timing has two effects
that move in opposite directions. It raises the private return to visible
effort, but it may pull effort away from less visible tasks that are more
important for team output. If output requires hidden contribution, earlier
recognition can reduce total output even when it increases visible activity.
